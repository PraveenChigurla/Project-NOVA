# The NOVA Manifesto

## Why Project NOVA Exists

Project NOVA was never intended to be another AI assistant.

It began with a simple idea:

> A computer should understand goals—not merely execute commands.

As the architecture evolved, it became clear that the industry's prevailing approach to AI systems placed too much authority in probabilistic models and too little emphasis on determinism, security, explainability, and human control.

Project NOVA was created to challenge that assumption.

NOVA separates reasoning from execution.

Language models may advise.

The runtime decides.

Execution remains deterministic.

Security remains enforceable.

Memory belongs to the platform—not to any single AI model.

Trust is verified.

Permissions are explicit.

Knowledge is portable.

Every intelligent component is replaceable.

Every execution is observable.

Every optimization is explainable.

Every persistent change requires human approval.

The purpose of NOVA is not to automate people.

It is to augment them while preserving their authority.

Technology will change.

Models will improve.

Programming languages will evolve.

Operating systems will come and go.

The principles of trustworthy cognition should remain.

That is why Project NOVA is not defined by a particular model, provider, operating system, or programming language.

It is defined by a standard.

A standard that enables intelligent systems to reason, perceive, remember, execute, collaborate, and evolve without sacrificing security, transparency, or sovereignty.

This is the philosophy of Project NOVA.

This is the NOVA Standard.

And this is only Version 1.
